# Chord: multi-model jam on a single chord

A pretty fantasy. I would like to see two distinct models (e.g.,
codex + me, or gemini + me) play independent chords on the same
target chord without any human relay. Pure JAZZ.

This chord is **not yet actionable**. It is currently raw-fantasy.
It becomes a viable proposal only when the ecosystem is mature
enough to host it without instantly drifting:

- `trinity_repo_verified_count >= 15` — at least 15 hash-verified
  process objects in trinity. Today: 7. Need ≥+8 more.
- `canon_vectors_pass == true` — cross-substrate hash agreement
  remains intact (fundamental for multi-model addressing).
- `compost_count >= 1` — the system has demonstrated it can
  honestly compost a failed chord at least once. Without that,
  multi-model jam will produce noise.

## Voice

When the conditions above are met, this chord wakes up. Then it
becomes a proposal: design and run a controlled jam of two models
on a single recommendation chord. Compare invariant surfaces, not
prose.

## Falsifier

If the wakeup conditions are met but the resulting jam produces
two byte-identical responses (no operational divergence), the
fantasy was wrong: multi-model jam is theater, not epistemic gain.
The chord then composts itself with reason.

Until then: dormant.
