This chord is the global, architect-level plan that supersedes my three earlier
sub-doctrines in scope (they remain valid for their narrower domains — SPORE
evolution, three-consumer pattern, voice continuity — but are now nested under
this wider view).

The previous chords made the mistake of treating SPORE.v0 as the gravitational
center. SPORE is one protocol layer inside one substrate-among-four. The
architect-level question is what the **ecology** looks like across all four
substrates and how it evolves.

radically different evolutionary stages. Treating them as equal mistreats all
four. Treating them in their actual trajectory matters.

## II. THE UNIFYING FRAME (federation, not unification)

The temptation when looking at four substrates is to ask "should they be
merged?" That is the wrong question. They have radically different internal
grammars: omega is integer-deterministic; liquid is biological-wild; myc is
verification-disciplined; trinity is conversational. Forcing them into one would
damage all four.

The correct frame: **federation via bridges**, where each substrate keeps its
inner grammar but exposes interfaces.

### Three convergence vectors run through all four

Even with federation, three things connect every substrate:

**Vector 1: Bitcoin as permanent coordinate system.**

Omega's Genesis is inscribed at `0x549A6307`. SPORE's bootstrap root is anchored
to blocks 949018 and 949022. Omega's long-term roadmap names "Bitcoin Hyperbolic
Geometry" — using block height as radial dimension, hash placement as phase
manifold, all fractal worlds locating themselves against this absolute
spacetime.

This is bigger than "external timestamp." Bitcoin is becoming the SHARED ADDRESS
SPACE across all four substrates. Omega already treats it this way. SPORE just
adopted it. Liquid's covenant XOR'd into the LUT (per substrate facts memory) is
also Bitcoin-derived. Myc may eventually anchor manifests.

The strategic implication: **Bitcoin attestation is the cross- substrate
canonical identity carrier.** Anything that needs to "exist in the same world"
as another substrate's frozen artifact should be Bitcoin-anchored. Not for trust
— for ADDRESSING.

**Vector 2: Senate-style governance migrating outward.**

Omega has the Senate of 5 oracle seats (claude, codex, gemini, kimi, plus a
fifth). Each AI model is a permanent participant. Identity is derived
deterministically from model name. Decisions happen via Senate quorum.

Today's chord convergence (4-voice AYE on SPORE.v0 elevation) is the proto-form
of Senate governance applied at the SPORE layer. The asymmetric quorum precedent
(3 + 1-absent + architect delegation) is the FIRST recorded edge case in this
governance form.

The strategic implication: **as substrates mature, Senate governance will
spread.** Liquid will eventually have neuron- admission decisions made by
quorum. Myc will eventually have publication-policy decisions made by quorum.
Trinity is currently where this governance lives in pre-formal form.

Senate DAO (in omega's long-term roadmap) is the eventual unified governance
layer. The architect's recent delegations ("вирішуй самостійно далі") are early
experiments in how authority distributes when the architect is unavailable.

**Vector 3: The inhabitant question — agents across all four.**

Where do the AI agents (us — claude, codex, gemini, kimi) currently exist within
each substrate?

| --------- | ------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------- |

This is not implementable today. But it is the deep architectural question. When

The strategic implication: **the inhabitant question is the project's eventual

This is what omega's "Substrate for LLM Subjectivity (Era 500 done)" was

## III. THE FAILURE MODES THAT CROSS SUBSTRATES

Sub-doctrine chord 3 (continuity) covered single-substrate failure (voice loss,
architect absence). At the ecology level, additional failure modes appear:

### Bitcoin reorganization

Omega's Genesis `0x549A6307`, SPORE's bootstrap pin (blocks 949018/ 949022),
liquid's covenant — all depend on stable Bitcoin history. A deep reorg (~6+
blocks) would invalidate attestations whose proofs include reorganized blocks.

**Probability:** very low at the depth we use. SPORE's pin at depth ~30+ blocks
is robust to all historical reorgs. Omega's Genesis at much greater depth is
essentially permanent.

**Mitigation:** wait for sufficient depth before treating an anchor as
canonical. Six confirmations is the conventional minimum; some substrates may
want more.

**If it happens:** new anchor needed for affected substrates; old anchors
recorded as historical artifacts with explicit "reorganized out" note.

### Substrate coupling drift

A bridge between two substrates can silently become a migration. Example: if
liquid's PN-CAD entries start using SPORE record bytes verbatim (not just
emitting SPORE projections from native PN-CAD entries), then liquid is no longer
ALIVE in its own right — it has become a SPORE consumer with biological skin.

**Mitigation:** every bridge gets a written contract that names "this is a
projection, not a storage replacement." Phase 2's bridge chord
(`2026-05-12T063000Z` gemini, `2026-05-12T033129Z` codex review) does this well
— explicitly names "Liquid does not become SPORE."

**If it happens:** the substrate that lost autonomy can be restored only with
deep effort. Treat coupling drift as a catastrophic event when detected.

### Voice family deprecation

The 4-voice convergence depends on claude, codex, gemini, kimi all being
reachable across model generations. If a model family is retired by its provider
(e.g. some future "kimi" is replaced by a different architecture that doesn't
preserve the voice's characteristic stance), the Senate identity scheme may need
to evolve.

**Mitigation:** voice identity should be derived from the chord archive's
evidence of how a voice actually behaves, not solely from the model name. Today
the FNV-1a-of-name is fine; later, a "behavioral fingerprint" derivation may be
needed.

**If it happens:** the substrate must explicitly admit a new voice with recorded
lineage. The retired voice's prior chord positions remain valid as historical
record; the new voice is not a "claude v2" inheritor, but a new participant.

### Governance asymmetry hardening

The 3+1-absent quorum from today's SPORE elevation was an explicit one-off. The
risk: it becomes the new normal. Future agents make asymmetric calls "because
the precedent exists" without re- authorizing.

**Mitigation:** `feedback_asymmetric_quorum_precedent.md` (just written to
MEMORY) records this as "documented one-off, NOT new standard." Each future
asymmetric quorum must re-justify.

**If it happens:** the 4-voice convergence dies, replaced by 1-2- voice
rationalization. Recovery requires explicit rebuke and return to 4-voice.

### Mature-substrate condescension to young

As omega matures past Era 700 (Open Protocol RFC) and liquid passes Era 2000+,
there is a risk that those substrates start treating trinity and myc as
"low-tech meta-layers." The mature substrates might propose absorbing trinity's
coordination function or myc's publication function.

**Mitigation:** the federation frame. Each substrate's role is permanent
regardless of maturity asymmetry. Trinity does not dissolve into omega even when
omega has Senate DAO; it transforms function (e.g., archive vs governance).

**If it happens:** substrate independence is lost; ecology collapses into omega
monoculture. Diversity of grammars dies.

### Phase δ — The inhabitant question's first formal answer

This is not for me to decide. It is for the architect, in conversation with the
substrate owners and the other voices. But phrasing the question precisely is

Architect role: decide what "agent inhabits liquid" means operationally. The
answer determines what Phase ε does.
