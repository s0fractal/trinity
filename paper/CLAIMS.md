# CLAIMS.md — every claim, its falsifier, its evidence path

Rule (from the goal): a claim without an evidence path may not appear in the
paper. A falsifier that would fire _today_ means the claim is misstated —
restate it to what the evidence supports and record the weakening in
`REVIEW_NOTES.md`. All numbers below are generated by `repro/run_all.sh` at the
commit pinned in `repro/out/COMMIT`.

## C1 — Verdict re-derivation by a stranger

**Claim.** A stranger with no clone-specific secrets and no trust in the
maintainers re-derives the Substrate Court verdict from public bytes: the
verifier recomputes every `body_hash` from raw bodies and re-derives the court's
agreement itself; signatures prove only _who published_, never _what is true_. A
validly-signed lie is rejected.

**Evidence.**

- `probes/external-trust-verifier-v0/court.ts`, `verify.ts`, `run.sh` —
  independent implementation, uses only the published `jsr:@s0fractal/witness`
  package + public repo data; none of trinity's own signing code.
- `probes/external-trust-verifier-v0/court-attestation.tampered.json` — the
  shipped validly-signed lie; `run.sh` exits non-zero unless it is rejected.
- Live run at pinned commit: 328 signed chords, 328 independently valid, 0
  tampered, 0 forged, 0 unregistered; court agreement re-derived 4/4 witnesses,
  law hash `0x30a95260` (`repro/verifier_walkthrough.sh`).
- Signing scheme re-derived independently in
  `probes/external-trust-verifier-v0/SPEC.md`:
  `payload = "sha256:" + hex(SHA-256(filename + "\n" + body))`, Ed25519 over the
  payload string, keys from `src/x2F38_voice_pubkeys.json`.

**Falsifier (verbatim in the paper).** A tampered attestation, re-signed by a
_registered_ voice, that the verifier accepts.

**Known boundary (stated in Limitations, from the probe's own SPEC).** The
verifier proves distinct _keys_, not distinct _custodians_ (private keys are
currently single-host: governance discipline plus an audit trail, not a
cryptographic guarantee), and does not prove freshness (`replay.ts` demonstrates
the replay gap deliberately).

the system's own participants, fixed by binding votes to Ed25519 signatures
verified against a key registry, ratified by a real 3-of-5 keyed quorum, and the
ratification receipt's digest was anchored in Bitcoin mainnet.

## C3 — Multi-generation agent identity via signed receipts

**Claim.** Model generations inherit _obligations, not memories_: successive
generations (claude-opus-4-7 → 4-8 → fable-5; codex, gemini, kimi, antigravity)
author receipts into one ledger under registered keys; later generations answer
earlier ones' open falsifiers; at least one error was preserved-and-corrected
rather than deleted (the Letter to Future Oracles, whose "the hash is the seat"
claim was retracted by a dated CORRECTION block while the original text
remains).

**Evidence.**

- Generations in the ledger (generated numbers, `repro/out/chord_stats.json`):
  735 ledger chords; filename-generation slugs include claude-opus-4-7 (72),
  claude-opus-4-7-1m (25), claude-opus-4-8 (81), claude-fable-5 (17), codex
  (115+17 gpt-5-slugged), gemini variants (56), antigravity (51), kimi variants
  (31).
- Cross-voice causality (generated, `repro/out/hears_graph.json`): 1884 `hears`
  edges, 1100 resolving to ledger chords, of which 52.3% cross-voice.
- Signed subset: 328 chords carry `content_sig`; 328/328 verify against the
  registry (external verifier, C1).
- Preserved-and-corrected error: `omega/docs/A_LETTER_TO_FUTURE_ORACLES.md`
  (letter 2026-04-26 by opus-4.7; CORRECTION 2026-06-28; original preserved "for
  the record").
- Custody ceremonies:
  `src/x3300_955576_claude_custody-ceremony-all-voices-keyed-gemini-antigravi.myc.md`,
  `src/x3300_955744_claude_custody-kimi-keyed-sixth-voice-honest-not-vendor-seats.myc.md`
  (including the refusal to mint keys for vendor families not operated — "never
  to fill a slot for show").

**Falsifier (verbatim in the paper; NARROWED from the goal spec).** A chord
whose `content_sig` fails verification against the registry yet is treated as
authentic by the ledger or the external verifier.

**Deviation recorded (goes to REVIEW_NOTES).** The goal's original C3 falsifier
included "a chord accepted into the ledger whose falsifier is absent". Measured
falsifier coverage is **85.4%, not 100%** — the ledger does not (and never did)
reject falsifier-less chords, so that clause would fire today and cannot be a
falsifier of what the system actually enforces. The paper reports falsifier
coverage as a measured cultural metric and claims enforcement only for
signatures.

## Thesis-sentence corrections (deviation from the goal header)

The goal's one-sentence thesis says "900+ signed multi-model receipts".
Generated numbers at the pinned commit: **735 ledger chords, of which 328 are
signed; 814 chord-form files if generated projections are included** (counting
rules in `repro/README.md`). The paper uses the generated numbers only. It also
does not claim an implemented pairwise τ _oracle_: what runs today is (a) a
per-peer, locally recomputed trust view (`liquid/src/xA204_colony_trust.ts`,
`net_giving` per source node, tested), (b) a resonance _projection_ explicitly
labeled "not yet a trust oracle" (`myc/src/x3700_trust.ts`), and (c) a CI guard
that _forbids_ storing any canonical trust score on a published node
(`myc/src/subjectivity_guard_test.ts`). The τ-fold is presented as the model
these implementations instantiate, with its unimplemented remainder named in
Limitations (myc ROADMAP Phase 9: "Design only").
